The vaults

The same engine, shaped to your work.

A vault is a single file on your device, holding one project. Its mode decides which sections appear and how the AI assistant is briefed. The engine, the private storage and the settings underneath stay the same across every mode. Four modes are ready today, and each one opens in a live demo right here.

Academic

For research. Point it at your Zotero library and every work turns into the claims, findings and methods it actually makes, each one carrying the exact passage and page it came from.

  • A graph of ideas rather than files, linked by supports, contradicts and refines.
  • Debates and open gaps surfaced from what your sources leave unsettled.
  • Deep Research reports whose every citation lands on a real item.
  • A writing copilot that runs inside Word and LibreOffice Writer.
Open the academic demo →

Study

For learning. Everything you study, kept in order by course and subject, with your own notes and materials underneath. No Zotero, nothing uploaded.

  • Organised by course, subject and topic, so your whole term sits in one place.
  • Tests and exams from your own material, ready to practise and mark yourself.
  • Ask the AI about your notes and it answers only from what you actually have.
  • Flashcards and spaced review built from your content, each one yours to approve.
Open the study demo →

Genealogy

For rebuilding people from primary sources. Family history is the obvious use, but the method fits any record work. Identity and kinship stay hypotheses until the documents prove them.

  • People, a family tree, a timeline and a map, with GEDCOM in and out.
  • An evidence archive of censuses, registers, letters and photos, each tied to the people inside it.
  • An AI genealogist that proposes kinship from the records and never adds a link you have not confirmed.
  • Conflicts stay visible, with period spellings and uncertain dates left as they are.
Open the genealogy demo →

Databases

For your own data. Fieldwork, corpora, catalogues, coding sheets. Typed tables you can genuinely analyse, without a spreadsheet flattening your categories into plain text.

  • Real column types for select, number, date, relation, rollup, formula and attachments.
  • The AI plans, the app computes. Correlations, chi-square, ANOVA and regressions run in code, not in a prompt.
  • Charts drawn straight from your rows, from heatmaps to scatter and box plots.
  • A data chat that answers over the real table and refuses to invent a value.

How it works

From your sources to ideas you can use.

Whatever you bring in, a research library, your own class notes, a recorded lecture, a box of old records or a table of data, Nodus reads it for the ideas and entities it actually holds. Then it connects them, and lets you ask questions that stay grounded in what is really there.

1

Bring in your material

Sync a Zotero library, drop in your own files, record a class or import a set of records. Nodus reads the content directly on your device, with nothing to upload and no plugin to install.

Zotero or your own filesAudio recorded and transcribedRecords and tables
2

It reads for meaning

An AI pass, in the cloud with your own key or fully offline, pulls out the claims, concepts, people or values each source holds and keeps the exact passage behind every one. Nothing is saved without the evidence for it.

Ideas & entities with page-level evidenceYour key, or run it offline
3

It connects, and answers

Ideas link into a living map, records line up into people and timelines, rows turn into charts. The assistant answers over that and only over that, so you can always trace a claim back to where it came from.

A map you can follow and questionAnswers you can trace to the source

Free & local-first, honestly

Completely free. Completely yours.

Nodus is open source under the MIT license, with no accounts, no subscriptions and no telemetry. Everything lives in a single file on your device. AI runs with your own key or fully offline through Ollama and LM Studio. It comes with encrypted automatic backups, a tamper-evident audit ledger, and a built-in server so your own AI tools can query your work locally.
